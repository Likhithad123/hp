How to change a Flat Tire

Roadside repair

It’s one of those things you hope not to have to do, but you never know when you might have to change a tire. Maybe you drive over a nail in a parking lot, hit some debris in the road or fall victim to a pothole – if you are on the road long enough, it is likely that you might find on the side of the road with a flat tire.
While it is true that you can call AAA or a tow truck for help, who knows where you’ll be when a flat tire occurs. You may not have cellphone service or too far away from the nearest tow truck. Knowing how to change a tire is a basic skill that every driver should know how to do.

Being Prepared

First, check your vehicle for a spare tire. In recent years, many car companies have eliminated the spare tire and jack from their models. Take a few minutes to look for the spare tire; don’t wait until you’re on the side of the road to find out that you don’t have one. If your vehicle didn’t come equipped with a spare, consider purchasing a tire, jack and lug wrench for your vehicle.
Instead of a spare tire, some vehicles come with a repair/inflator kit, while some luxury vehicles are outfitted with run-flat tires that are designed to work for a certain number of miles after being punctured. Both these options have pros and cons. For instance, a repair kit may not be much help for a sidewall puncture. Explore your options and pick the one that you feel most comfortable using in a roadside emergency situation.

WARNING

Be aware of your surroundings. If you're on a busy road, be particularly wary of vehicles driving by that might get too close.

Pull over to a safe spot

Find a flat, stable and safe place to change your tire.First you need to navigate your vehicle to a safe place to complete the repair.If possible, park your car away from traffic - a parking lot is an idael spot.You should have a solid, level surface that will restrict the car from rolling.If you have to be on the road,pull as far over on the shoulder as you can.Turn on your emergency flashers and set up your road flares or triangle reflectors.

2

Engage the parking brake

Once stopped,always apply the parking brake when preparing to replace a flat tire and put car into Park position in which it reduces the chances of your car rolling away.

TIPS

Store a couple of parking blocks in your trunk. Also store a roadside emergency kit in your trunk – most include a flashlight, tire sealant, tire inflator, reflective road markers, etc.

WARNING

For safety, place an object such as a log, large rock, etc., under the vehicle after jacking, but before removing the tire.

3

Place a wheel wedges

Place a heavy objects like Rock , Concrete , Spare wheels, etc or wheel wedges infront and back tires to further ensure the car doesn't roll while you fix the flat tire.
If your flat tire is at the front, put the wheel wedges behind rear tires and If you are changing the rear tire, place them infront of the front tires.
Just be sure that the heavy objects mentioned above are large enough to stop the car from rolling.

4

Gather Tools

Take out the spare tire and the jack.Locate everything you need and put them near the tire that you are changing. place the jack under the frame near the tire that you are going to change.
Ensure that the jack is in contact with the metal portion of car's frame.If you are not sure about the right place to put the jack , read you owner's manual.

TIPS

Familiarize yourself with the car owner’s manual on the proper procedure & warnings for changing a flat.

TIPS

Sometimes the wheels will seize to the hub, resulting in great difficulty in removal of the flat tire.
If this happens, you will need a sledgehammer and a 2x4 or other piece of wood to remove a seized wheel rim.

5

Take off the hubcups

If u have a hubcap or wheel cover,remove it.You can either use screwdriver or lug wrench to remove it.Skip this step if your lug nuts are exposed.
Make sure to loosen the nuts by turning it in counterclockwise direction.

6

Raise the jack

Raise the jack until it is supporting but not lifting the car.Later raise the vehicle so the tire is about 6" off the ground.The jack should be firmly place against the underside of the vehicle.
When lifting the car, if you sense any instability, stabilize the jack at proper position.Check to make sure that the jack is perpendicular to the ground.

WARNING

Don't get under the car when it's supported only by a jack.
If you're planning on working under the car, place it on jack stands, or drive it up on ramps if the wheels don't need to be free.

WARNING

Never use cinder blocks or makeshift jacks. A car is very heavy and if you use anything else you are putting yourself and your car at risk

7

Raise the car

Raise the car using jack about to 6" off the ground.You can do this either by pumping or cranking the jack.
After lifting the car remove the flat tire.It's always good idea to keep a samll jack stand in the vehicle as well.

8

Remove the lug nuts

Unscrew the lug nuts by hand ny turning them counterclockwise until they are loose. Repeat with all the lug nuts,then remove the nuts completely and set them aside in a safe place.

TIPS

If your wheels have locking lug nuts, be sure to keep the key-lug where you can easily find it. You will need it to change the tire.

TIPS

Rotating your tires at manufacturer's recommended intervals can prevent a common problem when changing a flat.

9

Remove the flat tire

Gripping the tire by the hands, pull it gently towards you until it's completely free from the hub behind it.
Place the flat tire under the vehicle so in the event of a jack failure the vehicle will fall on the old wheel, hopefully preventing injury.
The tire might stick due to rust. You could try hitting the inside half of the tire with a rubber mallet to loosen the tire.

10

Position the spare tire

Place the spare tire on the hub.Align the spare tire on the lug bolts.Take care to allign the rim of the spare tire with the wheel bolts, then put on the lug nuts. Push gently until the lug bolts show through the rim.

WARNING

Most spare tires (the undersized "donut" tires) are not designed for speeds of more than 50 mph (80 km/h) or for long distances.
Exceeding this speed can cause problems, including failure of the spare tire.

TIPS

When replacing nuts, be sure that the tapered side goes into the wheel. This centers the wheels and locks the nuts in place.

11

Screw the lug nuts

Grab the lug nuts and put them back on the lug bolts and tighten the nuts by hand. Later use the wrench to tighten the nuts as much as possible using a star pattern once after lowering the car to the ground.

12

Lower the car

Carefully lower the car without applying full weight on the car. Using the lug wrench, tighten the lug nuts by turning clockwise as much as possible.

TIPS

Remove the jack and wrench, assemble and practice operating it. If it is rusty, lubricate it.

WARNING

Be careful when hitting the hubcap with a rubber mallet so you will not ding or damage the new hubcap. If you fail to secure the hubcap correctly, you may lose the new hubcap while you are driving.

13

Replace the hubcap

If the hubcap you took from the flat tire will fit your spare, put it in place the same way you removed it initially.
If not, put it in a safe place in your vehicle.

14

Put all the tools

Put the old tire in your trunk and take it to a mechanic. Get an estimate for the cost of repair.
Small punctures can usually be repaired for less than $15. If the tire is not repairable, they can dispose of it properly and sell you a replacement

TIPS

Replace all the tools back in a proper place given to the respective tools.

TIPS

Check occasionally to make sure that your spare tire has sufficient air pressure (PSI).

15

Check tire pressure

Take a moment to ensure that the tire pressure in the spare tire is up to spec.
“T-Type” temporary spares, also called “mini-spares,” require 60 psi (420 kPa). If the tire needs pressure, drive (slowly) to a service station immediately.


Final thoughts

Your spare tire isn’t meant for long-term use, so your next stop should be the tire shop. The professionals will determine if your flat can be fixed or if it’s time for a new tire. Aside from taking your tire to a professional, the above procedure shouldn’t take more than 15 to 30 minutes to change a tire. Just be sure you don’t leave out any steps. Remember, practice makes perfect. Spend some time practicing changing a tire in the safety of your driveway. This ensures that you’ll be ready when it counts.
